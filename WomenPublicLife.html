Women in Public Life â€“ Safety Toolkit

Women in leadership, media, politics, activism, and content creation face unique digital threats. This toolkit provides awareness and protective strategies to help navigate online public spaces safely.

Targeted Harassment

Threat: Women face coordinated insults, threats, and intimidation meant to silence them.

Response: Use reporting tools, document incidents, block offenders, and enable public-figure safety modes.

Mis/Disinformation

Threat: False information can be spread to damage reputation or influence.

Response: Verify sources, issue statements, enable identity verification, and use fact-checking tools.

Impersonation & Deepfakes

Threat: Fake accounts and manipulated media can be used to discredit women.

Response: Watermark content, report impersonators, and educate audiences about deepfake detection.

Doxxing

Threat: Private data like home address or contacts may be leaked to endanger safety.

Response: Limit personal data sharing, use PO boxes, enable privacy filters, and contact authorities.

Gender-Based Hate Speech

Threat: Women face sexist, racist, and misogynistic attacks online.

Response: Use filtering tools, moderation settings, and pursue legal action when needed.
